All structural MRI scans underwent manual quality control assisted by the tool Qoala-T (Klapwijk et al. 2019). Qoala-T is a supervised learning tool that asseses accuracy of manual quality control of T1 imaging scans and their automated neuroanatomical labeling processed in FreeSurfer. It is particularly intended to use in developmental datasets, which was the case for the Leiden-CID MRI data.
